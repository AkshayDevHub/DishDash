Gutti Vankaya Curry

Image from Times Food

Description

Gutti Vankaya is a curried dish of stuffed eggplants from Andhra and Telangana cuisines. Small young brinjals are stuffed with a spicy ground peanut masala and simmered with onions, tomatoes, spices and curry leaves. It is a spectacular dish that’s made for a special meal and it tastes super delicious with a burst of flavors. It is eaten with hot rice and ghee or chapati.

Ingredients

For the Stuffing:

  • Small purple brinjals – 10–12
  • Dry coconut (copra), grated – 2–3 tbsp (or use desiccated coconut)
  • Roasted peanuts – 3 tbsp
  • Roasted sesame seeds – 1 tbsp
  • Coriander seeds – 1½ tbsp
  • Cumin seeds – 1 tsp
  • Dry red chilies – 4–6 (adjust to taste)
  • Garlic cloves – 4–5
  • Tamarind pulp – 1 tbsp (or small lemon-sized tamarind soaked in warm water)
  • Turmeric powder – ¼ tsp
  • Handful of coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt – to taste

For the Curry:

  • Oil – 3–4 tbsp
  • Mustard seeds – 1 tsp
  • Curry leaves – 1 sprig
  • Onion, finely chopped – 1 medium (optional, depending on regional variation)
  • Water – as needed
  • Coriander leaves – for garnish

Steps

  1. Wash the brinjals and remove stems.
  2. Make a "+" cut at the bottom of each brinjal, keeping them intact at the top (don’t cut all the way through).
  3. Soak in salted water for 10 minutes to remove bitterness (optional but recommended).
  4. Dry roast the following one by one until aromatic: Peanuts, sesame seeds, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, dry red chilies.
  5. Let them cool. Then grind them together with: Grated coconut, garlic, tamarind pulp, turmeric, and salt. Add very little water to make a thick, coarse paste.
  6. Gently stuff each brinjal with the prepared masala paste.
  7. Reserve the remaining masala (you'll use it in the curry).
  8. Heat oil in a wide pan.
  9. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  10. Add curry leaves (and chopped onions if using), sauté until soft.
  11. Place the stuffed brinjals gently into the pan in a single layer.
  12. Sauté on medium flame for 5–7 minutes, turning gently to avoid breaking.
  13. Add the leftover stuffing paste and a splash of water (about ½ to 1 cup).
  14. Cover and cook on low heat for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until brinjals are tender and oil starts separating.
  15. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with steamed rice or chapati.
