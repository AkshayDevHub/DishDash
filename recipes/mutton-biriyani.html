Mutton Biriyani

Image from freepik

Description

Mutton biryani is a fragrant, spiced rice dish made with mutton (goat meat or lamb), basmati rice, and a blend of aromatic spices. It's a popular and traditional South Asian dish, especially in Indian, Pakistani, and Bangladeshi cuisines.

Ingredients

For the Mutton Marinade:

  • 500g mutton (goat meat or lamb, bone-in pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Handful of mint leaves (chopped)
  • Handful of coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 2–3 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tbsp fried onions (birista)

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 mins)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2–3 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp oil or ghee

For Layering:

  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (or use yellow/orange food color)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 2 tbsp ghee or melted butter
  • Additional mint and coriander leaves

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the mutton with all marinade ingredients.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight gives the best flavor and tenderness).
  3. Bring water to a boil with whole spices and salt.
  4. Add soaked rice and boil until it's about 70% cooked (grains should break with some resistance).
  5. Drain the rice and keep aside.
  6. In a thick-bottomed pot (or handi), spread the marinated raw mutton evenly at the bottom.
  7. Layer the semi-cooked rice on top.
  8. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, coriander, saffron milk (or food color), and ghee/butter on top.
  9. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  10. Layer in an oven-safe dish, cover with foil, and bake for 45–60 minutes.
  11. Let it rest for 10 minutes after cooking.
  12. Gently mix before serving, being careful not to break the rice grains.
  13. Serve with raita, salad, or salan.
